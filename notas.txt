mientras que el Genoma es uno en todas las células del organismo en todo
momento, el Proteoma es un concepto mas variable. Lo es a varios niveles.

En un nivel estructural, el proteoma es variable en el sentido de que 
solo algunos genes se expresan en función del tejido en que se encuentren 
las células. Es esto precisamente lo que permite que a partir de un único
genoma puedan surgir tejidos y células diferentes en un organismo. También
a nivel subcelular, cada orgánulo es una estructura especializada

A nivel de estadío del desarrollo del organismo, el proteoma también 
puede sufrir cambios radicales. Esto puede entenderse bien en el caso
de insectos, organismos que se transforman radicalmente desde el estadío
de larva al estadío adulto.

Además existen niveles adicionales en el transcurso de la información gen-proteína:

múltiples proteínas diferentes en función de como se corten y unan los exones.

Analytical features of protein quantification techniques. Assay 
development indicates the facility of generating a quantitative assay
 for the protein(s) of interest, in terms of time, cost and labor. 
 
 Specificity of  a  protein  quantification  method  is  its  ability 
  to  discriminate  and  quantify  a particular protein sequence in a 
  mixture without interferences from the other components. 
  
  Limit of quantification (LOQ) is the minimal concentration or amount 
  of a protein that can be  confidently  quantified.  The  related  
  term,  limit  of  detection  (LOD),  is  the  smallest concentration 
  or amount of a protein that can be confidently detected.
 
 
  Precision  of a protein quantification method is the degree to which repeated
  measurements of the target protein(s) under unchanged conditions 
  (instrument settings, operator, apparatus and laboratory)  show  the 
  same  results,  within  a  short  interval  of  time.  
  Precision  is  affected  by random  errors,  and  unpredictable  fluctuations  
  around  the  true  value.Precision  can  be expressed  ascoefficient
  of  variation  (CV)which  corresponds  to  the  standard  deviation
  of repeated  measurements  divided  by  the  mean,  with  results  
  typically  expressed  asper  cent (%CV). The CV represents
  therefore a standardization of the standard deviation that allows to
  compare   the   variability   of   the   measurement   regardless   of
  the   magnitude   of   analyte concentration, in the working range of the assay.
  
  
  Accuracy of a protein quantification method refers to how close the
  average results are to the true quantity value. It is affected by 
  systematic biases that consistently affect the measurement in the same
  direction.  
  
  Single cell indicates the capability of the method to measure 
  the amount of a target protein at the  level  of  single  cells,  as
  opposed  to  the  measurement  of  the  average  protein  amount  in  a cell population.
  
  Localization  indicates  the  capability of the method to provide information
  on the (sub)cellular localization of the target protein. 
  
  Sample throughput is the number of specimens (samples) that can be 
  screened, over a given period of time, for the presence and/or the amount of the target protein
